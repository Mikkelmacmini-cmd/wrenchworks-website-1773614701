# AutoTrek — Concept A/B/C Art Direction Sets

## Goal
Create three distinct but production-friendly visual systems for Concepts **A**, **B**, and **C**, each with aligned guidance for imagery, icons, textures/backgrounds, gradient/material treatment, and do/don’t guardrails.

---

## Concept A — **Precision Trust**
A clean, modern, high-credibility direction built around confidence and clarity.

### 1) Imagery Style
- Bright, natural-light shop photography with real technicians and real bays.
- Framing: medium-wide environmental shots + close-up detail shots (hands/tools/components).
- Color behavior: neutral/cool base with selective warm accents (safety orange, brake-light red).
- Subject priority: “human expertise + transparent process,” not just cars.

### 2) Icon Style
- Thin-to-medium line icons (1.75–2px stroke), rounded corners, open shapes.
- Geometric consistency (same radius family, optical weight balanced across set).
- Two-tone optional accent for active states only.
- Recommended motif cues: shield, checklist, wrench, clock, map pin.

### 3) Texture / Background
- Mostly clean backgrounds with subtle micro-noise (1–2% opacity).
- Occasional faint blueprint/grid overlays in hero or section dividers.
- Off-white and cool-gray surfaces for readability.

### 4) Gradient / Material Treatment
- Soft linear gradients (navy → steel blue) with low contrast.
- Card surfaces: matte, low-elevation, minimal blur.
- CTA buttons: solid high-contrast fill (no chrome effects).

### 5) Do / Don’t
**Do**
- Prioritize legibility and trust cues.
- Use authentic, non-stock-feeling service imagery.
- Keep visual rhythm calm and structured.

**Don’t**
- Don’t overuse dramatic lens flares or heavy color filters.
- Don’t mix too many icon stroke weights.
- Don’t use glossy “dealership ad” treatments.

---

## Concept B — **Performance Energy**
A more dynamic, action-forward direction emphasizing speed, responsiveness, and momentum.

### 1) Imagery Style
- High-contrast photography with directional lighting and stronger shadows.
- Angled compositions and motion cues (rolling shots, kinetic diagonals, fast service moments).
- Color behavior: charcoal base + electric accents (red/orange/cobalt).
- Subject priority: “fast, expert execution.”

### 2) Icon Style
- Filled/duotone icons or bold mono glyphs.
- Slightly condensed, performance-inspired geometry (forward-leaning optical feel).
- Use motion marks sparingly for priority features (same-day service, rapid diagnostics).

### 3) Texture / Background
- Carbon-fiber-inspired micro pattern or diagonal brushed texture at very low opacity.
- Dark panels with subtle vignettes to focus attention.
- Background split blocks and angular separators.

### 4) Gradient / Material Treatment
- High-energy gradients (graphite → deep red, navy → cobalt).
- Materials can include subtle metallic sheen for premium-performance feel.
- Stronger contrast on CTA clusters and feature cards.

### 5) Do / Don’t
**Do**
- Use bold visual hierarchy and short, punchy section labels.
- Keep color accents disciplined (1–2 primaries + 1 alert).
- Pair dynamic visuals with concise copy.

**Don’t**
- Don’t let motion styling reduce readability.
- Don’t apply gradients to every component.
- Don’t use “racing” clichés (flames/checkers) literally.

---

## Concept C — **Neighborhood Reliability**
Warm, approachable, community-first direction with practical confidence.

### 1) Imagery Style
- Lifestyle-forward service photography: customer handoff, advisor conversations, family-safe outcomes.
- Softer light, warmer white balance, candid framing.
- Subject priority: “friendly experts who explain clearly.”

### 2) Icon Style
- Rounded, friendly outlined icons with slightly thicker stroke (2–2.25px).
- Optional contained-circle style for service categories.
- Small illustrative touches acceptable (spark/check/heart-shield) if consistent.

### 3) Texture / Background
- Warm neutrals and soft paper-like grain.
- Gentle rounded section containers; minimal hard edges.
- Optional local-map linework or subtle neighborhood pattern behind testimonials/contact.

### 4) Gradient / Material Treatment
- Warm gradients (cream → soft amber, slate → warm blue-gray).
- Material treatment: low-gloss, tactile, “printed brochure meets modern web.”
- Buttons and cards should feel approachable, not aggressive.

### 5) Do / Don’t
**Do**
- Lean into friendliness and clarity over technical bravado.
- Show diverse real customers + staff interactions.
- Use generous whitespace and calm pacing.

**Don’t**
- Don’t over-corporatize with sterile stock photography.
- Don’t use harsh black backgrounds or neon-heavy accents.
- Don’t clutter pages with dense texture.

---

## Cross-Concept Consistency Rules (applies to A/B/C)
- Keep service information hierarchy identical across concepts (for fair testing).
- Keep icon metaphor mapping stable (same icon meaning in every concept).
- Keep CTA copy and placement pattern consistent; style can vary.
- Maintain ADA-conscious contrast standards in all states.
- Use real automotive context imagery whenever possible; avoid generic business stock.

## Quick Selection Heuristic
- Choose **A** if priority is **trust, clarity, premium professionalism**.
- Choose **B** if priority is **energy, urgency, performance positioning**.
- Choose **C** if priority is **community warmth, approachability, family-safe reliability**.
